Istanbul Traffic Congestion — When and Where Is Traffic Worst?¶

Data: IBB Hourly Traffic Density Dataset — January to December 2024 (full year)
Question: When and where is traffic congestion worst in Istanbul, and how does it vary by season?

TL;DR: The Üsküdar (Bridge Approach) hotspot is the standout finding — averaging ~38 km/h year-round, roughly 20 km/h slower than the city average. Evening rush (5-7 PM) is consistently the slowest time across all seasons. The weekend advantage is modest (3.5% all-hours) but doubles when focusing on daytime hours only — nighttime free-flow data masks the real congestion relief. Seasonal variation is minimal: Istanbul has no true off-season.

# Download each month (only if not already downloaded)
for ym, url in months_2024.items():
    fname = f"data/traffic_density_{ym.replace('-', '')}.csv"
    if not os.path.exists(fname):
        print(f"Downloading {fname}...", end=" ", flush=True)
        for attempt in range(3):
            try:
                resp = requests.get(url, stream=True, timeout=600)
                resp.raise_for_status()
                with open(fname, "wb") as f:
                    for chunk in resp.iter_content(chunk_size=65536):
                        if chunk:
                            f.write(chunk)
                mb = os.path.getsize(fname) / 1024 / 1024
                print(f"OK ({mb:.0f} MB)")
                break
            except Exception as e:
                print(f"attempt {attempt+1} failed: {e}")
                if attempt < 2:
                    time.sleep((attempt + 1) * 5)
                else:
                    print(f"  FAILED after 3 attempts")
    else:
        print(f"{fname} already exists ({os.path.getsize(fname) / 1024 / 1024:.0f} MB)")

print("\nAll files ready.")

8. Key Findings¶

  1. Congestion is highly localized — the Üsküdar bridge approach is in a league of its own. This corridor (geohash cluster sxk9, Asian-side approach to the 15 July Martyrs Bridge) averages just ~38 km/h year-round — roughly 20 km/h slower than the rest of the city. It accounts for 6 million records in the dataset, making it the clear priority target for any congestion mitigation efforts.

  2. The evening rush is the worst time to be on the road. Traffic speed drops to ~52 km/h during 5-7 PM vs ~61 km/h in early morning — a ~15% slowdown. This pattern is consistent across all seasons, with winter evenings being the slowest overall.

  3. The weekend advantage is real but hidden by nighttime data. Including all 24 hours, weekend speeds are just 3.5% higher than weekdays because free-flowing midnight-to-dawn traffic (60+ km/h) is similar for both. When filtering to daytime hours (7 AM - 9 PM), the weekend advantage is roughly double — confirming that nighttime dilution masks the true daytime congestion relief.

  4. Istanbul traffic has no off-season. Monthly average speeds only vary by ~2 km/h across the entire year (55.3 km/h in December to 57.6 km/h in June). Unlike cities with seasonal holiday slowdowns, Istanbul's roads are consistently congested year-round.
